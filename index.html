My academic journey

I started my educational path in Zaatara Basic School, which is one of the oldest schools in the village, built in the early 1950s, until I graduated from Zaatara Girls Secondary School, which graduated many successful doctors, engineers, and students. I graduated with 98.7 in the scientific stream. Since I was young, I was among the outstanding students, despite my neglect of studies at times, but when I was determined to succeed and excel, I obtained my first certificate of appreciation in the tenth grade and I was among the top students in school. Thank God, not a semester passed after that without me being among the distinguished students and recipients of certificates of excellence. Then I decided to major in software engineering at Bethlehem University, for many reasons. and I'm now in my third year at the university, and thank God, since I entered the university, I have been and still am among the outstanding in many fields and I have participated in many programming competitions and external activities.

My City

I live in the village of Zaatara, south of Bethlehem, a small village crowned with olive and almond trees. Its houses are compact and close together. Every house has a story and every place has a story. Its people are simple and honorable, from whom we learned strength, manhood, pride and dignity. The doctor, engineer, soldier, teacher and farmer grew up there. They all struggled and worked hard despite the difficult circumstances, and we are all proud of them. Despite all the occupation's practices of abusing its people and destroying its landmarks, it is steadfast and patient. The occupation destroyed Al-Tahadi School several times, the last time was about two months ago and prevented its construction. Despite this, lessons are given to students in tents without a building. I am very proud to be a part of it and I pray to God to protect it from all evil... more

My Sports

Horsemanship

horseback riding grew in myself from a young age, for my love and admiration for the original Arab horse. They are ingrained in us as the origin of our language and our country. The Arabs have long been keen to teach their children to ride horses because of the great benefits of eleminatig the fear from their souls , courage and self-esteem. When I ride the horses, I feel like a bird flying over the sky of palestine with no limits and no restrictions.

Running

As for my running speed, it is a gift from God, and I participated in many races when I was young, such as cross-country racing.
I was always among the qualified in my school






Archery

As for my love for archery and my skill at it, it has grown with me since I was a child, playing with the children of our neighborhood when we would place the empty juice bottle on top of a rock a few meters away from us, then we would start aiming stones at them and damaging them, and the first person to break them wins. I was often the winner in this challenge, and because of my ability to aim accurately, I greatly improved my development in the sport of archery.
